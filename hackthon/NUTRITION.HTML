Nitrogen (N)

Boosts leafy growth and chlorophyll production. Deficiency causes yellowing leaves and poor growth.

Phosphorus (P)

Supports root growth and flowering. Deficiency causes stunted growth and purple-tinged leaves.

Potassium (K)

Improves drought resistance and fruit quality. Deficiency leads to leaf edge burns.

Calcium (Ca)

Essential for cell walls and root growth. Deficiency causes blossom-end rot and curling leaves.

Magnesium (Mg)

Main part of chlorophyll. Deficiency causes yellowing between leaf veins, especially in older leaves.

Sulfur (S)

Important for protein formation. Deficiency causes pale young leaves and reduced growth.

Iron (Fe)

Vital for enzyme function and chlorophyll. Deficiency results in yellowing between leaf veins (chlorosis).

Zinc (Zn)

Helps hormone regulation and enzyme activation. Deficiency leads to stunted growth and deformed leaves.
